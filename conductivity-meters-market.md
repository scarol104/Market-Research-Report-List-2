<p><p>Conductivity meters are widely used in various industries to measure the electrical conductivity of liquids. The market for conductivity meters can be classified into three types: portable, in-line, and bench-top. </p><p>Portable conductivity meters are compact and handheld devices that offer portability and convenience for on-the-go measurements. They are commonly used for field applications or in situations where portability is essential.</p><p>In-line conductivity meters are designed to be permanently installed in pipelines or systems, allowing for continuous monitoring and control of conductivity during production or industrial processes.</p><p>Bench-top conductivity meters are larger, stationary instruments commonly found in laboratories or research facilities. They provide accurate and precise measurements and are suitable for more controlled applications where reproducibility is crucial.</p></p>
